## You've completed the course

Six weeks. Thirty lessons. You've gone from not knowing what AI is to having a working agent on your phone.

## Map of what you've learned

**Week 1 — AI Fundamentals.** **Week 2 — LLMs.** **Week 3 — Infrastructure.** **Week 4 — Agents.** **Week 5 — Practical OpenClaw.** **Week 6 — Putting the agent to work.**

This is a solid foundation. You're not an expert — but you have the knowledge needed to use, configure, and maintain an AI agent with good judgement.

## Resources to keep learning

**Hugging Face NLP Course** (huggingface.co/learn): Excellent for diving deeper into how models work. Apache 2.0.

**fast.ai Practical Deep Learning** (course.fast.ai): One of the world's best Deep Learning courses. Creative Commons.

**DeepLearning.AI Short Courses** (deeplearning.ai): Short courses on specific topics: agents, RAG, fine-tuning.

**Anthropic Prompt Engineering** (docs.anthropic.com): Official prompting guide.

**OpenClaw GitHub** (github.com/openclaw): Official documentation.

## Project ideas

**Basic:** Personal daily briefing. Personal translator. Content curator.

**Intermediate:** Documentation assistant. Price monitor. Content generator.

**Advanced:** Multi-agent system. RAG with your own documents. Agent with access to business APIs.

## Contributing to the community

**Share this course.** The URL is public and free. **Report bugs** on GitHub. **Create skills** and share them on Clawhub. **Teach** — the best way to learn is to teach.

## Final words

AI isn't magic or science fiction. It's a powerful tool that, like all tools, is only as useful as the person wielding it. Now you have the foundation to use it well.

Don't try to know everything — the field is too vast and changes too fast. Focus on the tools that solve your real problems, and deepen from there.

Welcome to the world of agents.
